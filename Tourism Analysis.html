Introduction¶

This report presents analysis of tourism data. The company named "Visit with us" is interested in analyzing a viable business model to expand the customer base. A viable business model is a central concept that helps you to understand the existing ways of doing the business and how to change the ways for the benefit of the tourism sector. One of the ways to expand the customer base is to introduce a new offering of packages. Currently, there are 5 types of packages the company is offering - Basic, Standard, Deluxe, Super Deluxe, King. Looking at the data of the last year, we observed that 18% of the customers purchased the packages. However, the marketing cost was quite high because customers were contacted at random without looking at the available information. The company is now planning to launch a new product i.e. Wellness Tourism Package. Wellness Tourism is defined as Travel that allows the traveler to maintain, enhance or kick-start a healthy lifestyle, and support or increase one's sense of well being. However, this time company wants to harness the available data of existing and potential customers to make the marketing expenditure more efficient. The aim of this report is to analyze the customers' data and information to provide recommendations to the Policy Maker and Marketing Team and also build a model to predict the potential customer who is going to purchase the newly introduced package.

def clean_dataset(Tourism):
    assert isinstance(Tourism, pd.DataFrame)
    Tourism.dropna(inplace=True)
    indices_to_keep = ~Tourism.isin([np.nan, np.inf, -np.inf]).any(1)
    return Tourism[indices_to_keep].astype(np.float64)

The overall accuracy of the model is 88.0%. Since this is an imbalanced dataset, the overall accuracy doesnt give the correct measure of the models performance. In this confusion matrix, we can see that 0 is predicted 0 correctly 987 times whereas 1 is predicted as 1 only 105 times, rest 132 are wrong predictions. Considering this problem the primary aim is to find out the future customers who are likely to purchase the long term travel package (i.e prediction 1). The precision and recall for 1 are also not up to the mark. Thus one can see the model has got biased towards class 0, this is due to the imbalanced nature of the dataset.
